URANUS

The seventh planet from the sun.

Uranus’s blue-green color comes from the methane gas present in its cold, clear atmosphere. The dark shadings at the right edge of the sphere correspond to the day-night boundary on the planet. Beyond this boundary, Uranus’s northern hemisphere remains in a four-decade-long period of darkness because of the way the planet rotates. Scientists compiled this view of Uranus from images returned from Voyager 2 in 1986, when the probe was 9.1 million km (5.7 million mi) away from the planet. It takes 84 years to orbit (go round) the sun. With a diameter of 50,724 km it is 2,870,658,186 km away from the sun.
